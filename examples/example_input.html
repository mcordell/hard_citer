Housing
  • American Humane Certified: The American humane society has a certification for raising and handling animals{Anonymous:84QwNoh6}.
  • American Welfare approved: The Animal Welfare Institute has a certification for only family farmers raising livestock on a pasture. Standards are relatively strict and might comport well with the view of “traditional” farms{Anonymous:2009vy}.
  • Certified Humane is a NPO that also produces a label for humane treatment between birth and slaughter{certifiedhumaneorg:tw}.
  • A good comparison of the standards can be found here{Anonymous:2013vw}.
  • No Federal Law{Cohen:2009wj}.
  • California passed a state law in 2008 banning cages do not allow laying down or turn around freely{AnimalLegalandHistoricalCenter:ui}.
  • Fl, Or, Co, Az, Oh, Mi, Me, and Ri have laws banning gestation cages{Anonymous:7Z2x4Uu_}{Anonymous:2009vy}.
  • Many states have specific exemptions for farm animals in their animal cruelty laws, allowing animal husbandry practices to continue that would otherwise be deemed cruel{Tomaselli:2003vj}.
  • EU directive 98/58/EC sets up broad but vague standards for livestock welfare including language regarding the construction of housing, confinement, and adequate lighting{Directive:1998uk}.
  • EU directive 2007/43/EC established standards for broiler hen cages that greatly increased the required space per hen{Directive:2007ue}.
  • EU Directive 2001/88/EC bans use of gestational crates after Jan 1. 2013{Anonymous:5TIC-61i}.
  •  
  • At present there is not a treaty (binding or otherwise) concerning animal welfare.
  • The World Society for Protection of Animals has drafted several copies of the “Universal Declaration of Animal Welfare” which it has been petitioning the United Nations to adopt{Anonymous:TL22Q3WN}.

Transport
  • Industry publishes guidelines and auditing system for transport including, temperature, pen layout, and loading practice.
  • Livestock hauler audit based on Canadian Livestock Transporter Certification program{Grandin:2012uw}
  • Federal: 28 hour law: No animal shall be transported for 28 consecutive hrs. without a 5 hr. break for food and water{AnimalLegalandHistoricalCenter:uh}.
  •  
  • A European Commission published an opinion of Animal welfare transport during transport{Health:2011wh}.
  • This spurred increased and more stringent standards in the EC 1/2005 regulation{Anonymous:Rjuw_z3a}.

Slaughter
  • Guidelines based on the work of Temple Grandin, includes scoring system for handling and movement towards slaughter.
  • Focuses heavily on rendering the animals insensitive on the first bolting{Grandin:2012uw}.
  • Federal: The Humane Slaughter Act established that animals should be rendered unconscious before slaughter and preparation{Anonymous:1958wi}.
  • EU updated its slaughter standards with EC 1099/2009with a focus on greater training, regulation, and research. Some technical aspects of stunning and killing were defined{Anonymous:xvU3AxSo}.
